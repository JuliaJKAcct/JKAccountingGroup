# Script frameworks — hooks, structure, pacing, CTA, delivery

How to write a short-form script that holds attention **without** breaking the calm JK voice.
"Viral" here = clear, specific, and useful enough to share — never clickbait, never fear. The
Scriptwriter, Producer, and Social/Viral roles all pull from this file.

## The default spine: Hook → Problem → Insight → Proof → CTA
The primary structure for calm finance explainers. "Insight + Proof" carries the persuasion, so
you never need pressure.

| Beat | Time (in a ~30–40s cut) | Job |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | 0–3s | Open a curiosity gap in one line. Land by second 3. |
| **Problem / context** | 3–8s | Why it matters — the plain stakes, never doom. |
| **Insight** | 8–20s | The single idea or tip. One thing. |
| **Proof / payoff** | 20–25s | A real rule, sourced number, or mini-example that makes it land. |
| **Soft CTA** | last 3–5s | The natural next step (see CTA rules). |

Expand the **Insight** with one concrete example to stretch a 30s cut to ~60s. Don't add a second
idea to fill time — add depth to the one idea or cut the runtime.

**One idea per video.** Each script teaches exactly one thing or answers one question. Two ideas =
two videos. This is what lets the payoff land inside 30–60s and builds authority as a *curriculum*
of single-idea explainers rather than scattered multi-point clips.

Two alternates, for offer-driven pieces only:
- **PAS, softened** — Problem → *clarify the cost of getting it wrong* (calm consequence, never
  "Agitate"/doom) → Solve. Use when the video genuinely promotes something.
- **AIDA timing** — Attention 0–2s, Interest 2–8s, Desire 8–20s, Action last 3–5s. Only when the
  video is explicitly an offer. Otherwise default to the spine above.

## Hooks — first 3 seconds, 10–14 words, one line
The stay-or-scroll decision happens here. Pick the pattern that fits the topic; **never** a
fear/urgency/IRS-threat hook. If a scarier line is tempting, convert it into a curiosity or
correction hook instead.

| Pattern | Example | When |
|---|---|---|
| **Question / curiosity gap** (not yes/no) | "Why do most S-corp owners overpay themselves?" | The on-brand default. |
| **"If you…" self-selecting** | "If you pay yourself a random salary, watch this." | Filters to the right viewer, calmly. |
| **Myth-correction / contrarian-but-true** | "Most owners pay themselves the wrong way — and it's an easy fix." | Correction, not doom. |
| **Specific number** (sourced only) | "There's one number on your return that quietly sets your audit risk." | Only if the figure traces to a cited IRS source + year. |
| **Plain promise / value-forward** | "Here's the number the IRS actually looks at — and how to set it right." | States the payoff instead of teasing fear. |

Hook rules: one line · 10–14 words · lands by second 3 · no "Hey guys"/logo/intro · never
fear/urgency. The opening shot **is** the thumbnail, so the hook must work as text on frame one too.

## Pacing — write for the ear
- **Short declarative sentences,** one clause each, contractions, spoken cadence. Read it aloud;
  cut any word you wouldn't say out loud.
- **Open the loop** in the hook and don't resolve it until the payoff ("…and the fix is one line
  on your return — here it is").
- **One pattern interrupt** around the 25–35s drift point: a number on screen, a tone shift, a
  b-roll cut. One, not many.
- A slightly **slower, deliberate read** (~120–130 wpm) reads as more authoritative and is easier
  to follow for finance — and better for a bilingual audience reading along.

## Word budgets (aim the low end for breathing room)
At a calm education pace (~120–135 wpm):

| Duration | Target words |
|---|---|
| ~30s | ≈ 65 words |
| ~60s | ≈ 135 words |
| ~90s | ≈ 195 words |

On-screen text and b-roll beats reduce the words needed. **Always time the actual read aloud** —
and a Russian read will differ from these English figures when RU is added later.

## CTA — soft only
This is a professional-services firm; the CTA should feel like the natural next step after real
help, not an interruption. Ask little, trust the content:
- **Value-continuation:** "Follow — next week I break down how to actually set that number."
- **Low-friction resource:** "The plain-English checklist is in our bio."
- **Prompt a reply:** "Comment your question and I'll answer it."
Avoid hard "Book a call now." A soft CTA converts better here and stays on-brand. Match the CTA to
the intake goal (awareness → follow/save; lead-gen → resource/consult in bio).

## Delivery specs (carry these into the Flows brief)
The 2026 short-form standard is uniform across Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn:

- **Aspect ratio / resolution:** 9:16 at **1080×1920** (Full HD; 1080p is the max all four serve).
  Design the core inside a centered **~1080×1080 square** so it survives Instagram's 4:5 feed and
  1:1 grid crops.
- **File:** MP4, **H.264 + AAC**, 30fps (60fps only for high-motion), under ~4GB.
- **Duration sweet spots:** educational content **30–60s** (up to ~90s on Reels/Shorts when depth
  is needed). TikTok engagement peaks ~21–34s; Shorts ~50–60s beat sub-10s clips; LinkedIn
  awareness 15–30s. Platform ceilings (3+ min) are limits, **not targets** — long cuts get
  suppressed for new-audience reach.
- **Captions (mandatory):** default viewing is sound-off, so **burned-in** captions. Bold
  sans-serif, high contrast (white with a dark stroke/shadow), animated word-by-word, ~48–60pt+ at
  1080px width. **Produced in ElevenCreative Studio, not on the Flows canvas.** Also supply an
  `.srt` for LinkedIn.
- **Caption placement:** lower-**middle** third of the safe zone — not the absolute bottom (UI
  covers it), not the upper third (covers the frame-one hook).
- **Safe zones (satisfy all platforms):** centered ~1080×1080 core; keep text/faces/CTAs clear of
  ~250px top (status bar), ~400–450px bottom (caption/CTA block), ~150px right (like/share/comment
  rail).
- **Cover / first frame:** the opening shot is the thumbnail — high-contrast, in-focus. Provide a
  dedicated 1080×1920 cover; cover text 3–5 words, bold, ~48–60pt+, key elements inside the central
  square to survive the IG grid crop.
- **Caption copy & hashtags:** front-load the value/hook line in the post caption, end with the
  CTA. **3–5 targeted hashtags** (Instagram capped posts at 5 as of Dec 2025) — mix one niche tag
  (<500K posts) with one broader; place them in the caption.
- **LinkedIn caveat:** 9:16 video **ads** are delivered to mobile only and may be skipped on
  desktop. If LinkedIn desktop ad reach matters, also produce a 1:1 or 4:5 variant; organic 9:16 is
  fine for mobile-first LinkedIn.

> Engagement percentages floating around creator blogs (3-second retention, caption lift, etc.) are
> directional benchmarks, not peer-reviewed research. Use them to justify the *shape* of the script,
> not as facts in the script itself.
