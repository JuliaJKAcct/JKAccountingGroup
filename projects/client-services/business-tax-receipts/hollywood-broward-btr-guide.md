## The big picture: this is usually a two-tier license

To operate legally in Hollywood you generally need **two** receipts, obtained in
this order:

1. **Broward County Local Business Tax Receipt** (county) — the City of Hollywood
   application **requires an active Broward County Tax Collector account**, so get
   the county receipt first (or alongside).
2. **City of Hollywood BTR** (municipal) — submitted with the county account,
   entity docs, licenses, proof of location, and a **Certificate of Use** *unless
   the address is single-family residential or a condominium* (see the CU note
   below).

Depending on the business activity, you may **also** need state-level items
(entity registration, sales-tax account, a professional license). Those are in
§ "State & federal items" below.

> **Why the order matters (Hollywood specifically):** the City of Hollywood BTR
> application lists "a current and active account with the Broward County Tax
> Collector" as required documentation — so for Hollywood, the **county** piece
> comes first, then the city application ties it together. (Some other Broward
> cities sequence it the other way; always read the specific city's page.)

## City of Hollywood — City BTR + Certificate of Use

**What it is:** the city-level license to operate at a specific Hollywood
address, applied for online through the City's Local Business Tax Receipt portal.

**Required documentation (from the City's application page):**
1. Signed **lease or proof of ownership** (property tax bill, warranty deed, bill
   of sale, insurance certificate, or utility bill).
2. Copy of **County, State or Federal licenses** where applicable.
3. A **current and active account with the Broward County Tax Collector** — i.e.,
   do the county receipt first (see below).
4. **Fictitious Name Registration** (if using a DBA) and/or **Articles of
   Incorporation** (if a corp/LLC), from `sunbiz.org`.
5. **Certificate of Use permit** — **except for single-family residential and
   condominiums** (see the CU note below).
6. **$25.00 non-refundable** application processing fee **+ the local business tax
   fee** (full amount determined when the application is submitted/reviewed).

The application ends with a checkbox certifying you gathered all required
documentation and assume responsibility for its accuracy — incomplete
applications are not accepted, so assemble everything first.

**Certificate of Use — do you need one?**
- For a genuine **home-based business** (single-family home or condo where the
  owner **lives** and runs the business as a secondary use), the City exempts the
  CU, and Florida's Home-Based Business Act (Fla. Stat. **§ 559.955**) protects
  the use, subject to reasonable standards (parking, signage, no external evidence
  of the business, no heavy customer traffic).
- ⚠️ **The "house used only as a business" case:** if **no one lives** at the
  address and it's used **100% commercially**, that is *not* a home occupation —
  it's a commercial use of the property. The single-family exemption is only about
  the CU paperwork; it does **not** confirm the use is allowed there. Confirm the
  **parcel's zoning** permits the commercial use (check `bcpa.net` and City of
  Hollywood zoning) *before* relying on the exemption.

**⚠️ VERIFY (City of Hollywood):**
- The **local business tax** amount for the specific classification (the $25

- Whether the specific address/zoning permits the intended use (see CU note).
- **Where:** City of Hollywood — Local Business Tax Receipt portal on
  `hollywoodfl.org` (Business → Local Business Tax Receipt) · City Hall, 2600
  Hollywood Blvd, Hollywood, FL 33020.

## Broward County — Local Business Tax Receipt (do this first for Hollywood)

**What it is:** the county-level receipt, required in addition to the city's. A
**separate receipt is required for each location and each category of business**
you conduct.

**How to apply:** online via **BTExpress** —
`broward.county-taxes.com/btexpress`. Processing time is **24–48 hours**.

**Requirements (from Broward County Tax Collector):**
- For regulated businesses, present a current **state license, Certificate of
  Competency, registration, FL Seller of Travel License, or exemption letter** —
  the names/info must match the application.
- **Tangible Personal Property Tax:** if the business has furniture, equipment,
  or other tangible items valued at **$25,000 or more**, a TPP return (form
  DR-405) is due to the Broward County Property Appraiser by **April 1**.
- If the business were in *unincorporated* Broward it would need a **county**
  Certificate of Use from Building Code Services first — **not applicable here**,
  because Hollywood is an incorporated city (its CU, if any, comes from the city
  step above).

**Contact:** Broward County Tax Collector — Local Business Tax, **954-357-4829**.

## State & federal items (only what the activity requires)

Not every business needs all of these — the **activity** determines it:

- **Entity on Sunbiz** — LLC/Corp registered with the FL Division of
  Corporations (`sunbiz.org`). If operating under a name different from the legal
  entity name, file a **Fictitious Name / DBA** (Fla. Stat. § 865.09).
- **Federal EIN** — from the IRS (needed for the entity, payroll, and most BTR
  apps).
- **Florida sales & use tax** — register with the FL Dept. of Revenue (**Form
  DR-1**) if the business sells/rents taxable goods or certain taxable services.
  Register for **Reemployment Tax** too if there are employees.
- **Professional / industry license** — e.g. restaurants & food service (DBPR
  Division of Hotels & Restaurants / county health), cosmetology & salons (DBPR),
  construction/contractors (state or county competency), childcare (DCF). The
  county and city both want to see this before issuing the BTR.

## Fees, renewals & deadlines

- **Business tax year:** October 1 – September 30. Receipts **expire Sept 30**;
  renew before then. Delinquent renewals carry escalating statutory penalties
  (Fla. Stat. § 205.053) — **⚠️ VERIFY** the current penalty schedule when late.
- **Half-year rule:** a **first-time** receipt obtained **on or after April 1**
  is charged **half** the annual tax (Fla. Stat. § 205.0535 / § 205.053). Useful
  for a business starting mid-year.
- **Fees are per receipt** — remember it's city **and** county, and one per
  location/category.

## Common pitfalls

- **Skipping the city step.** People apply to the county and forget the City of
  Hollywood CU + BTR (or vice-versa). Both are required.
- **Wrong classification.** The activity code drives the fee and whether a state
  license is required — get it right at intake.
- **Regulated activity without the state license.** The BTR won't issue until the
  professional license/competency card is in hand and the names match.
- **Home-based assumptions.** Still needs a CU/BTR even though the city can't ban
  it.
- **Missing the Sept 30 renewal**, then paying penalties.
