What this means

In plain words — and what it does not mean.

  • The IRS uses a day count. All of this year's days, plus one third of last year's, plus one sixth of the year before. Reach 183 (and at least 31 days this year) and you usually file as a resident.
  • It changes what you report. A tax resident reports income from the whole world to the IRS. A non-resident usually reports only US income. Getting this wrong is a common and costly mistake.
  • This is about taxes, not your visa. It does not change your immigration status or your green card. And some visas, and treaties between countries, can change the answer.
