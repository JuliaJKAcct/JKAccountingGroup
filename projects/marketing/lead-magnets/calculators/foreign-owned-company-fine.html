Why this is easy to miss

In plain words, so you know if it is you.

  • "My company made no money, so I have nothing to file." Not true here. This form is about the money that moves between you and your company — even the money you put in to start it counts.
  • It applies to a normal single-owner LLC too. If a foreign person owns a US LLC, the IRS treats it as a company for this one filing. Most owners have never heard of it.
  • The good news: the form itself is free. It is just information. Filing it correctly and on time is all it takes to avoid the $25,000. If past years were missed, there is often a way to fix it.
