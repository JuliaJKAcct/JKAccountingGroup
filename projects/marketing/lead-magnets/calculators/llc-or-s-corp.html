What this really means

In plain words, so you can decide with your eyes open.

  • Self‑employment tax is 15.3%. It pays for Social Security and Medicare when you work for yourself. Everyone who runs a business pays it.
  • An S‑Corp splits your money in two. You take a fair salary, and the rest is business profit. You pay that 15.3% on the salary — but not on the rest. That is where the saving comes from.
  • It is not free. An S‑Corp costs a little more to run — payroll and an extra tax return, usually a few thousand dollars a year. That is why it is worth checking your real numbers before you change anything.
