# Video 2 — Choosing Your Lead Magnet

**Track:** 4 — Lead Generation & Nurture · **Module:** Lead System Setup
**Type:** Actionable — has homework

## Core definition

A lead magnet is a free resource offered in exchange for contact info. The
objective: give a **full solution to a narrow problem**, for a **highly
specific client avatar** — not a broad, generic freebie.

**Key principle:** the lead magnet determines who you attract. "5 tips for
tax season" attracts literally everyone who feels tax-season stress (i.e.
nobody qualified). "Job quoting calculator for landscape construction"
attracts landscape contractors specifically. Narrow and specific = qualified
traffic; broad and generic = unqualified traffic.

## The three types (in increasing effort/value)

1. **Cheat sheet** — "top 5 tips" / "how to X" format. Fast to produce (minutes
   with AI), but reads as low-value/generic ("I could've Googled this").
   **Not recommended** — the instructor doesn't build these for clients.

2. **Calculator** — a purpose-built spreadsheet/Google Sheet solving one
   specific calculation for one specific avatar (e.g. short-term rental deal
   analyzer, fix-and-flip profitability calculator, contractor job-costing
   calculator). User plugs in green cells, red cells auto-calculate. Works
   well because it's tangible, immediately useful, and easy to make hyper
   specific to a niche. **Recommended starting point** — best ratio of
   build-effort to perceived value.

3. **Assessment / quiz funnel** — a form (e.g. Typeform) that asks several
   qualifying questions and routes the person to a personalized result page
   (e.g. "you're/aren't ready for X," or an AI-generated personalized report
   of top strategies for their situation). Highest effort and highest
   personalization → generally highest lead quality. Best suited for paid ad
   traffic specifically. Result page still funnels into the VSL / book-a-call
   step.

## Homework from this video (the instructor's own action items)

1. **Workshop the lead magnet** — brainstorm ideas, starting from a
   calculator: what's a task that normally takes your ideal client ~30
   minutes that a tool could cut to ~2 minutes?
2. **Build it** (cheat sheet / calculator / assessment — calculator
   recommended as the starting point).
3. **Build the funnel around it** — VSL, landing page, email sequencing (the
   pieces from Video 1).

## Relevance to JK — candidate lead magnets (shortlist, none built yet)

JK's actual niche is narrower than "small business owners": per
[`positioning.md`](../../../positioning.md) it's **foreign-owned business
founders in the US**, whose core fear is "I don't know the rules here" (tax,
compliance, local regulations), often compounded by a failed attempt to
bridge the gap with a home-country bookkeeper who doesn't understand the US
system. The shortlist below was built by mapping specific daily worries this
avatar has — that JK can actually solve — to a tool, not by picking generic
accounting topics. See "Writing principles for these assets" in the project
[`README.md`](../../README.md) before drafting real copy for any of these.
