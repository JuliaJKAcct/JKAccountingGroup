A quick financial gut-check

Is Your Business Financially Set Up the Way It Should Be?

Businesses evolve — revenue grows, teams change, priorities shift — but the financial setup underneath doesn't always keep up. Run through this quick gut-check — if it's all squared away, great. If anything gives you pause, that's worth a conversation.

01Entity & tax structure

You may have set up as an LLC years ago. Now that the business is bigger, is that still the most tax-efficient structure — or are you leaving money on the table because an S-Corp election was never made?

02Bookkeeping

Are your books actually current and accurate — or is there a backlog you keep meaning to catch up on? Clean books are the difference between real decisions and educated guesses.

03Registrations & licenses

Are your registrations still current — federal, state, and local? It's easy for a renewal or a filing to quietly lapse once a business has been running a while.

04Sales tax (if applicable)

If you collect sales tax, is it being remitted correctly and on time? Rates and rules can shift as a business changes — worth confirming you're still doing it right.

05Payroll

Is your payroll compliant — people classified correctly (W-2 vs. 1099), filings current? This is a common place things quietly slip as a team grows.

06Estimated taxes

Are your quarterly payments actually being made, in the right amount? As revenue and costs shift, the numbers change more than people expect.

07Compliance & open items

Any IRS or state letters sitting unanswered, filings missed, or deadlines you're not fully sure about? These are the things that don't get smaller by waiting.

08Reporting to grow

Can you see, month to month, whether the business is actually making money? Or is it one lump number you can't really steer by?
