# Realtor referral playbook — how to actually offer this

> **Purpose.** The checklist alone doesn't work — a realtor handed a PDF with
> no script and no timing either forgets it or fumbles the moment. This is
> what to brief the realtor on: **when** to bring it up (there's more than
> one right moment), **what to say**, and the **hand-off** that gets JKA
> into the loop. Pairs with the checklist in this folder
> (`operating-business-financial-checklist-en.html` / `-ru.html`) — the "is
> your business set up the way it should be?" gut-check, built for a business
> **that already exists** and is moving into a new space. That's who a
> commercial realtor actually meets: an owner relocating or expanding, not
> someone starting from zero. (For the rarer client who's genuinely just
> starting their first business, the Pre-Launch
> `new-business-launch-checklist-*.html` fits better — see the notes at the
> end.) Strategy behind this: `../strategy.md` § "Standardized front-offer
> chassis" and § "Real estate affiliate channel".

## Channel: text/WhatsApp, not email

This audience doesn't run on email. **Text or WhatsApp is the default
channel for everything below** — sending the checklist, and the hand-off to
Julia. Treat email as a rare exception (a client who specifically prefers
it), not the fallback. Every script below is written to be said out loud or
sent as a short text — nothing here should read like a formal email.

## The 3 things the realtor needs

1. **When** — realtors don't all hit the same moment in the same
   conversation, so pick whichever fits rather than forcing a script into
   the wrong spot.
2. **What to say** — short enough to say from memory.
3. **What to do after** — the hand-off, same day, by text/WhatsApp.

## 1. When — four moments, pick what fits

Not every deal reaches "lease signing" as a clean, separate moment — some
close over a few conversations, some finish on-site with keys handed over
right away. Use whichever of these actually happens in the realtor's own
process:

**A — During the search, the moment the client talks about their business**
(often the earliest and most natural — a client says they're relocating,
expanding, opening a second location, or "finally getting a real space").
No need to wait for signing; planting it early means it lands before they're
buried in move-in logistics.

**B — Right at lease signing / keys handover** (the most common single
moment — the deal is done, the client is in front of them).

**C — When the client asks a logistics/licensing/permit/tax question the
realtor can't fully answer** (a natural pivot, not a forced pitch — "that's
actually more of an accounting question" is a genuine, honest handoff, not
a sales move).

**D — A short follow-up text a few days after keys, if none of the above
came up** ("hope the new space is coming together — sending that checklist
I mentioned").

The realtor should use **one** of these, whichever the conversation
actually offers — not force all four into a single meeting.

## 2. What to say — one script per moment

Casual, spoken, no notes needed — and the through-line is *the realtor
lending their own credibility* ("I put it together," "my clients found it
helpful"), which is what actually makes a referral land:

**A — Mid-search, business comes up:**
> "Oh nice, congrats on the move. You know, whenever you're settling into
> the new place, I've got a little checklist I put together with an
> accounting firm I work with — a lot of my clients found it really helpful,
> just the financial side worth a quick look when you're moving. I'll send
> it over, no rush."

**B — At signing / keys handover:**
> "Congrats on the new space! Before I forget — take a look at this
> checklist. I put it together with an accounting firm I work with, and a
> lot of my clients have found it really helpful. It's basically the
> financial stuff worth double-checking when you're moving into a new place.
> If anything on it looks unfamiliar, give them a call — they do a free
> consultation."

**C — Client asks a question the realtor can't fully answer:**
> "Honestly, that's more of an accounting question than a real estate one —
> but I work with a firm that handles exactly this for my clients. Want me
> to connect you? They do a free consultation."

**D — Short follow-up text, a few days after keys:**
> "Hope the new space is coming together! Meant to send you this — a quick
> financial checklist I put together with an accounting firm I work with. A
> lot of my clients found it helpful. Free consultation if anything looks
> unfamiliar."

Whichever moment it is, the ask is the same: hand over or text the
checklist, and say a version of "they'll walk you through it if anything's
unclear." **The realtor never has to explain the accounting content
itself** — their job stops at the hand-over.

## 3. The hand-off (this is the step that's usually skipped)

Same day, by text/WhatsApp, not batched or left to an email thread:

> "New referral: {{CLIENT_NAME}}, {{PHONE}}, prefers {{EN/RU}} — moving
> into a new space at {{PROPERTY}}."

**Why this can't be optional:** without it, the referral depends on the
client reaching out on their own — and this audience won't, especially by
email. JKA reaching out first, within 1 business day, is what actually
turns a hand-over into a call. This is the point where "gave them a
checklist" becomes an actual referral — skip it and nothing downstream
happens.

(The checklist does have a **scan-to-WhatsApp QR** built in, so a motivated
client can reach us straight off a printout — but treat that as a bonus, not
a replacement for the hand-off. The hand-off is what makes JKA reach out
*first*, which is the part that actually converts.)

## Sending the checklist itself

- **In person:** hand over a printed copy if one's on hand — cleanest, most
  natural, no extra step. The scan-to-WhatsApp QR on it means even a
  printout is one tap from a conversation.
- **By text/WhatsApp:** share the PDF/link directly. A one-line message is
  enough — don't write a formal note to go with it:
  > "Here's that checklist — [file]. Free consultation if anything's
  > unclear, just let me know and I'll connect you."
  > (RU: «Вот тот чек-лист — [файл]. Если что-то будет непонятно, есть
  > бесплатная консультация — скажите, и я вас свяжу.»)
- **Email:** only if the client has specifically indicated they prefer it —
  don't default to it. Keep it just as short as the text version, no formal
  letter:
  > Subject: A quick checklist for your new space
  >
  > Hi {{CLIENT_FIRST_NAME}} — attaching a quick financial checklist from my
  > accounting contact, JK Accounting Group. Worth a look as you settle into
  > the new space. Free consultation if anything's unclear — I've passed
  > your info along, or reach out directly: julia@jkaccountinggroup.com.
  >
  > (RU subject: «Небольшой чек-лист для нового помещения») Hi
  > {{CLIENT_FIRST_NAME}} — короткий чек-лист от моего бухгалтерского
  > партнёра, JK Accounting Group. Стоит посмотреть, пока обустраиваетесь на
  > новом месте. Бесплатная консультация, если что-то непонятно — я
  > передал(а) ваши контакты, либо пишите напрямую:
  > julia@jkaccountinggroup.com.

## Notes for whoever briefs the realtor (Julia/team)

- This playbook is the thing to walk the realtor through once, in person or
  on a short call — not something to send cold and hope they read. Two
  minutes of "here's the moments to watch for, here's what to say, here's
  how to text me the contact" is the actual onboarding.
- **Pick the right checklist for the client.** Default to the **Operating**
  one (`operating-business-financial-checklist-*.html`) — the realtor's
  typical client already runs a business and is just changing address. Only
  reach for the **Pre-Launch** launch checklist
  (`new-business-launch-checklist-*.html`) for a client who's genuinely
  starting their first business.
- Pick the EN or RU checklist file to match the language the realtor used
  with the client.
- When the referred contact comes in, treat it like any other lead: reach
  out within 1 business day, and the call itself walks through the
  checklist live rather than asking the client to have filled it out.
- Referral fee is owed only if the free call converts into a paying
  engagement (flat fee, not a percentage) — see `../strategy.md`.
