Added value for your clients

Buying or leasing commercial space isn't just about picking a location. That's usually the moment a business owner runs into a lot of questions.

  • Do I need to change my company structure?
  • Should I elect S-Corporation status?
  • How should my bookkeeping actually be set up?
  • When do I need to register for sales tax?
  • How do I set up employees correctly?
  • What expenses can I actually deduct?
  • How do I get ready for taxes?

Most of these questions fall outside what a realtor can be expected to answer. That's exactly where we come in.

How it works

If a client's deal raises questions about bookkeeping, taxes, or how their business is financially set up — just introduce them to us.

We're glad to do a 15–20 minute introductory conversation, answer general questions, and point them in the right direction.

This isn't a sales pitch, and it isn't a full tax consultation. The point of the call is simply to help the owner understand what's worth paying attention to right now.

If it turns out they need deeper analysis, tax planning, or ongoing bookkeeping support after that — we'll separately discuss what an engagement could look like.

When it's worth recommending us

Opening a first location
Relocating the business
Opening a second or third location
Buying commercial property
Expanding the company
Tax or bookkeeping questions
Questions about setting up employees
Choosing a business structure

What you can say to the client

I work with an accounting firm that specializes in supporting small businesses. If you have questions about taxes, bookkeeping, or the financial side of your business, they're happy to give you 15–20 minutes to answer general questions and point you toward what to watch for.

That's really all it takes. We'll take it from there.

How to introduce us

The simplest way — start a group chat, or just send us:

  1. Client's name
  2. Phone number
  3. Preferred language

We'll reach out to the client directly and set up a time to talk.

Why this works well for you

  • You're not taking on responsibility for answering accounting or tax questions.
  • Your client gets access to a vetted specialist.
  • It lets you offer a higher level of service and builds client trust.
