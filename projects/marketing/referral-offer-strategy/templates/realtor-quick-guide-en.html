Bringing Up the Checklist With a Client

Four moments where this naturally fits — use whichever one shows up in the conversation. You never need to explain the accounting side; just hand it over and say we'll take it from there.

1
While they're still looking

Oh nice, congrats on the move. You know, whenever you're settling into the new place, I've got a little checklist I put together with an accounting firm I work with — a lot of my clients found it really helpful, just the financial side worth a quick look when you're moving. I'll send it over, no rush.

2
Right at signing / handing over keys

Congrats on the new space! Before I forget — take a look at this checklist. I put it together with an accounting firm I work with, and a lot of my clients have found it really helpful. It's basically the financial stuff worth double-checking when you're moving into a new place. If anything on it looks unfamiliar, give them a call — they do a free consultation.

3
When they ask something you can't fully answer

Honestly, that's more of an accounting question than a real estate one — but I work with a firm that handles exactly this for my clients. Want me to connect you? They do a free consultation.

4
A quick follow-up, a few days later

Hope the new space is coming together! Meant to send you this — a quick financial checklist I put together with an accounting firm I work with. A lot of my clients found it helpful. Free consultation if anything looks unfamiliar.

One more thing

Text or WhatsApp Julia the same day with the client's name, number, language, and the property — that's what actually turns a hand-off into a referral:

"New referral: [name], [phone], prefers EN/RU — moving into a new space at [property]."
