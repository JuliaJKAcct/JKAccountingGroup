# Talk track — the marketing-partner conversation

> A plain-language script for walking a marketing-agency owner through
> [`one-pager.html`](./one-pager.html). Written for someone who doesn't love
> "selling" — because you're not selling, you're bringing him a way to make his
> own clients more successful and his own service stickier. Peer to peer.
> Hand him the page, talk it through, let him react.

**The whole thing in one sentence (your north star):**
*"Before your clients spend on growth, we make sure the business keeps what it creates — and we both grow from it."*

---

## Before you walk in

- **You're not the vendor here — you're a partner.** He refers to people he
  trusts; the goal of this meeting is for him to trust *this*, not to close a deal.
- **Let him talk.** Your best line all meeting is *"where's this wrong?"* People
  buy in to what they help shape.
- **Don't lead with money** — not the $500, not referral fees. Lead with his
  clients winning. The money takes care of itself.
- Have the one-pager printed (or on screen). Swap `[Partner Firm]` for his firm's
  name first — it should feel made for him.

---

## The opener

> "I've been thinking about how we could actually help each other's clients — not
> just send names back and forth, but build something. I put one page together.
> Can I walk you through it? And tell me where it's wrong."

That's it. Low pressure, collaborative, hands him the page.

---

## Walking the page

**The "why" (top of the page).**
> "Here's the pattern I keep seeing. A client spends real money on marketing, it
> works, more revenue comes in — and they *still* feel broke, or they get hit with
> a tax bill nobody planned for. Not because the marketing failed. Because the
> business underneath wasn't set up to *keep* the profit. That's the gap I want to
> close for your clients — so your results actually stick."

**What their clients get (the three steps).**
> "So here's what I'd offer them. Step one is free — a quick five-minute financial
> check, and *you* hand it out as part of your onboarding, with your name on it. It
> just shows a client where money could leak once they scale. Most people find one
> or two things they didn't know.
>
> If they want to go deeper, that's the $500 check — a real look at their actual
> numbers with a written plan. And here's the part I like: if they come on with us
> monthly, that $500 comes right back off their first month. So for a serious owner
> it's basically risk-free."

*(Let the third step — ongoing — speak for itself. Don't pitch the monthly. The
ladder does that for you.)*

**Two ways we work together.**
> "We can run this two ways. With your *new* clients, it's just part of how you
> onboard them. With the clients you *already* have, we host a few casual Zoom
> sessions together — marketing and money, side by side. Your whole client base
> gets real value, and honestly, it's a warm room for both of us."

**What's in it for him.** *(Say this part plainly — it's the reassurance.)*
> "And this isn't me asking you for a favor. You get something to market — 'every
> client gets a financial readiness check with our partner firm.' Your clients keep
> more and stay longer, which makes *your* work look better. And it goes both ways —
> my clients need good marketing too."

---

## The close (keep it small)

> "I don't want to overcomplicate it. Easiest way to start: pick five of your
> clients, and I'll run the free check with them. You'll see if it's valuable before
> we ever talk about anything bigger. What do you think?"

One clear, tiny next step. Then stop talking and let him respond.

---

## Likely questions — easy answers

| He asks… | You say… |
|---|---|
| "What does it cost me?" | "Nothing. It's co-branded to you, I do the work." |
| "Will you hard-sell my clients?" | "The opposite of how I work. The free check just shows them what's there — if they want more, they ask. I'd rather keep your trust than get one pushy referral." |
| "So what's in it for you?" | "The clients who want ongoing help become mine. That's it. The free part is just how they get to know me." |
| "How much of my time is this?" | "For new clients, almost none — you just include the check in onboarding. For the Zoom series, four short sessions we plan together, and I do the heavy lifting on content." |
| "My clients aren't your Russian/Ukrainian audience." | "Doesn't matter for this — the check and the sessions work for any owner. The bilingual part is just a bonus for the ones who need it." |
| "Can I see the check first?" | "Of course — I'll send it over. Fill it out yourself, takes five minutes." |

---

## What NOT to do

- Don't open with price or referral splits.
- Don't pitch the monthly retainer — let the free → $500 → ongoing ladder pull.
- Don't stack three next steps. One: the five clients.
- Don't fill silences. Ask "where's this wrong?" and let him talk.
