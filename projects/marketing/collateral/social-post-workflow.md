## What it's for

A repeatable flow for producing Instagram / Facebook posts for JK Accounting
Group that are: **accurate** (verified facts), **educational**, **100% on-brand**,
and at the **right size** for each network — combining the best of each tool.

## Who does what

- **Claude (here):** topic strategy, the copy/text (EN/RU), the source material,
  the "image brief" or prompt, and **the final on-brand finish** at each network's
  size (a publish-ready PNG). Verifies the facts.
- **Nano Banana / Gemini or NotebookLM** (Google apps, in the browser): generate
  the **illustration or infographic** when a post needs an AI-created image. This
  is what Julia already pays for.
- **Julia:** decides topics and approves; generates the images in the Google apps;
  publishes.

## The workflow, step by step

1. **Topic.** With Claude we decide the topic, the angle, and which client
   question/need it answers.
2. **Content package (Claude).** Claude delivers:
   - **Post copy** — headline, body, hashtags, CTA (EN/RU).
   - **Source material** — the educational content, with verified facts.
   - **Visual brief** — an exact description of the visual we want + a **prompt**
     (for Gemini) or a **source package + infographic instruction** (for
     NotebookLM).
3. **Visual route (decide).** See the decision guide below. Three routes: Claude
   directly · Gemini (prompt) · NotebookLM (grounded).
4. **Generate the visual.** Julia generates it in the chosen app (or Claude does
   it directly if it's a diagram).
5. **Brand finish (Claude).** Julia brings the visual; Claude produces the final
   version: brand (colors/type/logo from the brand guide), **exact and legible
   text and numbers**, and the **exact size** for the network → PNG.
6. **Review and publish.** Checklist: on-brand? facts verified? bilingual correct?
   right size? → publish.

## Decision guide: Gemini, NotebookLM, or Claude directly?

Your instinct is right: for **complex, educational tax topics**, **NotebookLM is
usually better** than a loose prompt in Gemini. Here's why, and the rule:

| If the visual is… | Best tool | Why |
|---|---|---|
| A **dense educational infographic** on a complex topic (with real, professional content) | **NotebookLM** — give it study materials (your explanations, IRS docs) | It is "grounded in sources": it builds the visual from real material, so it comes out **richer, more accurate, and more educational**. It invents less. |
| An **illustration, scene, metaphor, or stylized "hero" image** (no critical text) | **Gemini (prompt)** | Fast, iterative, style control. Ideal when the exact text isn't the point. |
| A **clean diagram with exact labels/numbers**, or the **final asset** | **Claude (me)** — directly | Perfect, legible, bilingual text, 100% on-brand. Often you don't even need image AI. |

### Golden rule (critical for a tax firm)

> **Never publish numbers, figures, or terms read from text the AI wrote *inside*
> an image.** Image AIs spell text badly and invent data. The correct data is
> placed by Claude/Julia as **verified text** in the final finish. (This matches
> the brand guide: *"real facts only — if a number or quote can't be verified,
> omit it."*)

**In practice it's almost always a combination:** NotebookLM/Gemini provides the
visual richness or the illustration → **Claude** adds the brand layer + the exact
text + the size. The AI provides "how it looks"; Claude ensures "what it says" and
"that it's our brand."

## Sizes by network (reference)

- **Instagram feed:** 1080×1080 (square) · **1080×1350 (vertical 4:5 — best for
  infographics)**
- **Instagram Stories / Reels:** 1080×1920
- **Facebook feed:** 1080×1080 or 1080×1350 · link 1200×630
- **Carousel:** several cards at 1080×1350
