## 1. The strategic takeaway

What wins in accounting short-form right now is **credibility delivered inside creator-native
mechanics**: a curiosity/identity hook in the first 3 seconds, **one** takeaway per clip,
burned-in captions (most viewers watch muted), a 20–45s runtime, and **myth-vs-reality** or
*"is this legal? yes, but…"* framing that rides trending misconceptions.

The biggest tax creators (Karlton Dennis, Duke Moore, Navi Maraj, Vivian Tu) win on
shock-numbers, loophole energy, and personality — but **that lane is now saturated, distrusted,
and under regulatory scrutiny** (the IRS 2025/2026 *Dirty Dozen* names social-media tax advice;
~$162M in penalties across ~32K returns; FINRA's first finfluencer fine). That backlash is the
opening for a calm, licensed firm: a **documented debunk / myth-correction lane** is being
pioneered by licensed pros (e.g. Jasmine DiLucci) precisely because audiences burned by hype now
reward accuracy.

**So the play for JK:** adopt the **structure** (hook, pacing, captions, one real number per
clip) but never the **content** (no secrets, no shock-$0, no fear). JK's calm senior-advisor
voice and **bicultural RU/UA wedge are the differentiator**, not a handicap — slower cadence and
"here's who it does and doesn't apply to" honesty convert in a post-hype feed.

**Timing (it's July 2026):** filing-season breakouts (car-loan interest, the OBBBA deductions)
have cooled from their Jan–Apr peak. The right move now is to **build the evergreen library and
the myth-correction franchise ahead of Q4 year-end planning and the 2027 filing season** — a
roughly **70/30 evergreen-to-timely** mix.

> **Honesty note:** demand throughout is *qualitative* — no per-topic view counts were verifiable,
> so none are asserted. Direction is well-supported by breadth of authority coverage and IRS/press
> signals. **Every on-screen figure must be re-verified against IRS primary sources on production
> day** — rates are year-indexed and several OBBBA provisions are temporary (2025–2028) with MAGI
> phase-outs.

## 2. Delivery playbook (adopt the mechanic, not the guru tone)

**Hook (first ~3 seconds).** No logo, no "hey everyone" — open cold on the point or the tension.
Use only the **calm** hook types: *Identity Call* ("If you own an S-corp, this is for you" / "For
foreign founders who just opened a US LLC") and gentle *open loop / myth-correction*. Skip
aggressive contrarian/confession framings that tip into guru energy. Calm recast of a shock hook:
open on a **real sourced fact**, not a secret.

**Structure (one idea, ~20–45s).** hook (0–3s) → the myth/claim (3–8s) → why it's wrong / the
real rule (8–25s) → what to actually do (25–33s) → soft CTA. Up to ~60–90s only on Reels/LinkedIn
and only if every beat earns the next. **One takeaway per clip.**

**The two flagship formats:**
- **Myth-vs-reality correction** — state the viral claim plainly, correct it calmly with the real
  rule, then **one** limit. JK's natural home.
- **"Is this legal? Yes, but…"** — validate the strategy, then teach the guardrails creators skip
  (documentation, fair-market rates, genuine business purpose). Perfect for Augusta rule,
  hiring-your-kids, vehicle write-offs.

**Also strong:** anonymized **client-scenario mini-stories** (~6–7× more memorable than a bullet
lecture — and JK's bicultural audience is ideal for culturally-recognizable scenarios);
**client-question Q&A** ("A client asked me: can I write off my car? Here's the honest answer");
and **one-question-one-clip search titles** ("Can a foreigner get an EIN without an SSN?") so
YouTube/Google surface them against literal queries.

**Craft rules:**
- **Sourced dollar anchor per clip** as the memory peg — cold-open on "$25,000" (Form 5472
  penalty) or "$20,000 AND 200 transactions" (1099-K), then explain. Never invent a figure.
- **Pattern-interrupt every 2–3s** (cut/zoom/text-card); every named number cuts to a full-screen
  card. Keeps a calm talking-head from feeling static without hype.
- **Burned-in word-by-word ("karaoke") captions**, brand accent, high-contrast, bottom-third safe
  zone. 80%+ of social video is watched muted — non-negotiable.
- **Credentials-forward trust signal** in a lower-third/bio: "US CPA · foreign-founder specialist
  · educational, not advice." The IRS itself now tells taxpayers to check preparer credentials.
- **Always close educational-not-advice.**

**Platform tuning (same 9:16 clip, tuned CTA):** TikTok/Shorts for cold reach (reward 3s-hold +
completion/shares); Reels for authority (reward saves/DM-sends — CTA "save this before tax
season"); LinkedIn for referral trust (founder talking-head, sound-off, CTA "Founders — did
anyone explain this when you set up your LLC?"). Cadence ~**3–5 clips/week**, one script
repurposed. Optional subtle trending-audio bed on TikTok/Reels ducked under the voiceover; **skip
audio trends on LinkedIn's mute-first feed.**

**Bilingual = code-switch, not translation.** Keep the hook/frame in Russian or Ukrainian but say
IRS form names in English verbatim (Form 5472, W-8BEN, ITIN) and caption the exact term so
viewers can search it themselves. Re-cut top English performers as RU/UA drop-ins.

### 1 · Debunking viral bad tax advice — *the flagship franchise*
**Why now:** highest-fit, lowest-risk pattern for an accuracy-first brand, and the only
opportunity rated high-confidence on demand. The debunk format renews every season and every new
viral hack; the IRS put bad social-media tax advice on the Dirty Dozen and tied ~$162M in
penalties to it, so audiences are primed for a calm licensed counter-voice — and the lane is still
under-served by credible pros.
**JK angle:** debunk the myths that specifically snare foreign-born and RU/UA founders — "open a US
LLC and pay zero US tax" (ignores ECI, Form 5472, state tax, resident vs non-resident), "no income
means no filing" (Form 5472 $25K penalty), fake self-employment credits circulating in immigrant
Telegram/Facebook groups, "write off your car 100%." Calm framing: *the person in the video isn't
lying to hurt you — they're oversimplifying, and the oversimplification is what costs you.*
**Hook:** "This tax tip went viral — and for foreign business owners, it's quietly incomplete."
**Format:** 25–45s green-screen react beside a **paraphrased, muted** screenshot (paraphrase the
claim; don't repost a face). Myth struck through, real rule highlighted. Optional RU/UA caption.
**Compliance:** every "real rule" sourced to IRS.gov/statute; say "oversimplified/incomplete,"
never "scammer/fraud"; no reposting a creator's face without rights; no fear-bait; no unverifiable
view counts.

### 2 · LLC vs S-corp & the "reasonable salary" question
**Why now:** durable high-intent demand with a mild Q1/Q4 lift, and it maps directly onto JK's
actual deliverable (the reasonable-compensation service/skill). A dense field of creator content
plus active CPA rebuttals to "$0 salary / 50-50 / 60-40" myths = a wide-open calm-correction lane.
**JK angle:** the edge isn't explaining the S-corp (saturated) — it's the **eligibility gate**
creators skip: a **non-resident alien cannot hold S-corp stock** (IRC 1361(b)(1)(C)); one
non-resident owner voids the election, but a **resident alien** (green-card / substantial-presence)
can. Then "reasonable salary comes **first**, tax savings second," and the rough profit thresholds
(~$40–50k break-even, clearly worthwhile ~$75–100k). Funnels to JK's reasonable-comp work.
**Hook:** "Everyone online loves the S-corp. First check whether you can even elect one."
**Format:** 35–50s calm talking-head with lower-thirds + a saveable checklist card; EN base + RU/UA cut.
**Compliance:** don't conflate "foreigner" with "ineligible" (resident aliens *are* eligible); no
universal dollar salary or 60/40 · 50/50 split as valid; thresholds are general ranges, not IRS
figures; S-corp is a federal *election* (Form 2553), not a legal-entity change; reclassification
risk (Watson, Barron) stated calmly, not as fear; any savings example labeled illustrative.

### 5 · OBBBA paycheck myths — "no tax on tips" & "no tax on overtime"
**Why now:** campaign-famous phrases people badly misunderstand; law through 2028 (recurs each
season); documented mass audiences (25M+ filers claimed the overtime deduction). Most content
targets hourly W-2 workers — the **employer/payroll-owner angle is under-served**.
**JK angle (two audiences):** for owner-clients running payroll — "your employees are already
asking": you do **not** stop withholding, you **do** have a new reporting job (tips: Box 14 for
2025; overtime: Box 12 code TT for 2026), and neither applies to their own S-corp distributions or
1099 contractors. For W-2 earners in the RU/UA community (salon/beauty, restaurant, barbers, nail
techs, rideshare) — correct "all my tips/overtime are now tax-free": tips is a **deduction up to
$25,000** of voluntary tips in IRS-listed jobs; overtime is only the FLSA premium "half," capped
$12,500/$25,000; **FICA still applies to both.**
**Hook:** "'No tax on tips' doesn't mean tax-free. Here's what it actually means."
**Format:** 30–45s myth-vs-reality with "still true" cards; one clean example ($20→$30/hr = only the
$10 premium counts); EN + RU/UA; LinkedIn carousel for owners on the reporting change.
**Compliance:** say "deduction," never "tax-free"; tips capped $25K, listed occupations only,
auto-gratuity excluded, FICA still applies, 2025–2028; overtime = FLSA-required premium only, W-2
only (not 1099 / S-corp distributions), caps $12,500/$25,000, phases out $150k/$300k MAGI; nobody
stops withholding; stamp "as of 2026."

### 6 · Vehicle / business-car write-offs
**Why now:** the single most stitched/corrected tax topic in short form — durable year-round with
Q4 and Jan–Apr spikes. Perfect standing input for the debunk franchise.
**JK angle:** the calm licensed correction to a claim the audience has already seen. Bicultural
hook: many RU/UA founders come from countries where a company car is normal — "yes, a business
vehicle can be deductible here, but the IRS applies three tests: real active business, over-50%
business use, documented mileage." Accuracy edge: **don't repeat the stale ~$31K SUV cap** — under
OBBBA, 100% bonus depreciation is back for vehicles placed in service after Jan 19, 2025, so a
legitimate high-business-use heavy SUV really can be written off heavily year one. The honest
sharper point: the limiter isn't the sticker price, it's whether business use is real and provable

**Hook:** "A business vehicle can be deductible in the US — but only if these three tests pass."
**Format:** 30–45s talking-head; three lower-thirds (real business / over-50% use / written mileage
log) → the honest twist → educational-not-advice card; pinned RU/UA comment.
**Compliance:** exact-year figures; verify OBBBA bonus-depreciation before any "full write-off";
GVWR + >50%-use conditions; recapture warning; don't repeat the stale SUV cap; never tell anyone to
buy a vehicle to save tax.

imply no form = no tax; name lower-threshold states as changeable examples; don't conflate with
1099-NEC/MISC.

### 9 · $6,000 senior deduction (65+, 2025–2028)
**Why now:** the "no tax on Social Security" misconception is nationally documented and spreading —
SSA issued a formal correction; IRS/Forbes/Tax Foundation/CNBC published corrections. Runs through
2028.
**JK angle:** the eligibility fine print national creators skip: it needs a **valid SSN and US
tax-resident status** — ITIN filers and nonresident aliens are excluded, which hits many older
immigrant relatives; and it's a per-person deduction that phases out on MAGI, so an adult child
advising an elder parent needs the real thresholds, not the "seniors pay no tax now" rumor in
family chats. JK = the calm bilingual advisor who lets a founder confidently correct the myth for
their parents.
**Hook:** "No, seniors did not stop paying tax on Social Security. Here's what actually changed."
**Format:** 30–45s myth-bust with three calm cards; optional RU/UA caption track.
**Compliance:** never say/imply "no tax on Social Security"; it's a **deduction, not an exemption**;
$6,000 per eligible person (up to $12,000 MFJ if both qualify); 2025–2028; phases out above
$75k/$150k MAGI (gone $175k/$250k); needs valid SSN + resident/citizen status.

### 12 · SALT cap $10K → $40K (OBBBA, 2025–2029) + the PTET pairing
**Why now:** verified across many outlets; heavy coverage signals real search appetite, but acute
interest peaked over the 2025 season and has cooled. Rated moderate — saturated, and the headline
homeowner benefit is a weak fit for JK's Florida base.
**JK angle:** do **not** run "homeowners save $40K!" (only ~0.4% of FL homeowners exceed the cap; no
FL state income tax). Two differentiated angles: (1) bicultural plain-language — it's a **deduction
that lowers taxable income, not a refund/check**, and only helps if itemizing beats the standard
deduction; (2) the real money — OBBBA left the state **PTET** (pass-through entity tax) workaround
intact, so S-corp/pass-through owners can often "stack" the personal cap with an entity-level PTET
election. Flag the cross-border wrinkle: clients with property/income in NY/NJ/CA benefit far more
than pure-FL residents.
**Hook:** "The SALT deduction just jumped to $40,000 — here's who actually benefits."
**Format:** 45–60s "who this helps / who it doesn't" talking-head; LinkedIn numbers carousel.
**Compliance:** deduction, not a $40K credit/refund; only helps itemizers; FL has no state income
tax — don't imply otherwise; phases down over $500k MAGI; temporary ($40k for 2025, +1%/yr through
2029, reverts to $10k in 2030); PTET varies by state — "confirm with your advisor."

### 13 · QBI made permanent + 100% bonus depreciation restored (OBBBA)
**Why now:** QBI/199A is among the most-searched small-business terms and "made permanent" is

deduction and full first-year equipment write-off are now stable, so hiring/equipment/salary

**Hook:** "The tax break you thought expires this year? It just became permanent."
**Format:** 30–50s talking-head with lower-thirds; dense dollar figures in the caption with a year;
LinkedIn-first.
**Compliance:** **QBI stays at 20% permanently — never say 23%** (an earlier House draft that did
not become law); 100% bonus depreciation for property placed in service **after Jan 19, 2025** —
state the date; Sec. 179 base $2.5M (2026 indexed ~$2.56M — attach a year); QBI has income
thresholds/SSTB limits — always "if you qualify"; no "you'll save $X."

### 14 · 1099-NEC threshold $600 → $2,000 (payments on/after Jan 1, 2026)
**Why now:** confirmed OBBBA change touching every client who pays contractors; low fabrication
risk. Live for 2026 payments but no forms filed until Jan 2027 — queue for Q4 2026 → Jan/Feb 2027.
**JK angle:** own the two things generic posts skip. (1) Foreign-owner cut: RU/UA founders paying

others differ (Missouri ~$1,200, Arkansas ~$2,500) — so "you can stop 1099-ing under $2,000" is
dangerously incomplete.
**Hook:** "The 1099 rule just changed — but skipping the form doesn't mean skipping the tax."
**Format:** 30–45s talking-head with lower-thirds; saveable old-vs-new card; EN now, RU later.
**Compliance:** effective date is 2026 payments (not the 2025 filing season); all income still
taxable without a form; don't conflate with 1099-K; state thresholds vary — present as changeable;
foreign-contractor W-8BEN/1042-S track is separate.

- **Ukrainian-language cuts specifically** (not lumped under "Russian-speaking") — the scan found
  little dedicated UA US-tax content. Likely a genuine gap; confirm before claiming "first."

## 5. Series concepts (franchises JK could own)

1. **"Real rule or myth?"** — the flagship debunk franchise. Paraphrased viral claim → "here's the
   part it skips" → the real rule in one sourced sentence → what it means for a business owner like
   you. Ends educational-not-advice.
2. **"New to the US"** — numbered evergreen series, one narrow foreign-founder decision per clip.
   Numbering compounds saves; each English pillar is a drop-in RU/UA re-voice.
3. **"Founder's tax question of the week"** — LinkedIn-native, Julia to camera, one anonymized real
   client question answered plainly in 15–30s.
4. **"Two cultures, one tax code"** — the explicit bicultural franchise: "here's how it works back
   home, here's how the US does it, here's the first correct step."
5. **"Boring, legal, and it has rules"** — the calm counter to "how the rich pay less tax": one real
   structure per episode with its eligibility catch on screen.
6. **"Deadline desk"** — short calendar-pegged reminders published 2–3 months ahead of each peak so
   search indexes them in time. Predictable, repeatable, low-production.

## 6. What to avoid

- **Shock-number / "they paid $0" / "insider loophole" hooks** taken literally — hype that implies
  illegitimate strategy and breaks the calm voice. Borrow the "concrete real number first"
  *structure* only, with a sourced figure.
- **Any "secret / loophole / hack / the IRS doesn't want you to know / one weird trick" framing** —
  the most saturated, distrusted lane (roundups cite up to ~87% of finfluencer advice as potentially
  misleading).
- **Fear-based audit framing** ("the IRS will destroy you," jail-time thumbnails, panic countdowns).
  Reframe as calm reassurance (~1 in 200).
- **The "don't worry, it's a write-off" flex** and "write off your car/vacation/haircut" as true —
  legally wrong; correct with business-use %, Section 179, listed-property rules.
- **Get-rich-quick / wealth-flexing aesthetic** — documented audience fatigue and the exact
  "unqualified finfluencer" association to avoid.
- **Fabricating or borrowing social proof** — never invent view/follower/"as seen" numbers or show
  unsourced on-screen stats. Every figure real and sourced.
- **Stale/wrong figures in circulation** — $600 as the current federal 1099-K threshold, 23% QBI, or
  the ~$31K SUV cap as current. Re-verify every OBBBA figure against IRS.gov; stamp "as of 2026."
- **Evergreen pillars built on temporary provisions** (tips, overtime, car-loan interest, bonus
  depreciation) without stating the 2025–2028 sunset and MAGI phase-outs — keep timely and evergreen
  separate.
- **Merging immigration-status consequences with tax obligations, or status as fear-bait** — the
  hardest rule in immigrant-facing content.
- **Mocking the debunked creator** — correct the idea, say "oversimplified/incomplete," never repost
  a face without rights.
- **Exact-dollar "you'll save $X" promises and universal ratios** (60/40, 50/50 splits) — use ranges
  and conditions; label examples illustrative.
- **Letting trending audio/formats drag the voice into a jokey/hypey register** — adopt the mechanic,
  not the guru tone; skip audio trends on LinkedIn.
- **Treating creator-tool benchmarks** (3s-hold %, completion %, caption-lift %) as guarantees —
  directional, not audited; test against JK's own analytics.
