Executive summary

The bottom line

A reasonable W-2 salary for the owner of Solux Media Corp for 2025 is $65,900, with a defensible range of $61,000–$85,000. The figure is built from what the Miami market would actually pay to replace each of the five roles the owner performs — priced at the low, well-corroborated end of live market data. The owner's current salary of $42,000 sits below this range while distributions of $128,000 run well above it: the pattern the IRS most often challenges. We recommend raising the salary to roughly $65,900.

Methodology

How we reached the number

We used the cost, or "many-hats," approach: an owner-operator rarely does one job, so reasonable pay is what it would cost the open market to replace each function they perform. We split the owner's 2,200 annual hours across the roles they actually fill, priced each role by triangulating several independent wage sources, and summed the weighted result. Each rate is set at the low end of the corroborated market cluster — never below it, and only below the government (BLS) figure where at least three market sources agree.

Why the number is lower than a BLS-only report

For the two manager roles, the government/BLS wage runs far above what Miami employers actually pay — roughly $157k vs a ~$75k market for marketing managers, and ~$101k vs a ~$62k market for operations managers. A BLS-only build-up would have produced a salary near $92,000. Triangulating to the corroborated market low lands at $65,900 — about $26,000 less wage base — while remaining defensible because multiple independent sources support each figure.

Reasonableness cross-check

Current salary vs. recommended

● Under-compensated — increase recommended

The current $42,000 salary is only 25% of the owner's total cash ($42k salary + $128k distributions = $170k) and sits below the reasonable range. A low salary paired with large distributions is the exact fact pattern the IRS recharacterizes (Watson, Fleischer). We recommend raising the salary to $65,900. The business can support it: net income before owner comp is $185,000, leaving roughly $119,000 available for distributions after the recommended wage.

Assumptions & limitations

What this analysis assumes

Figures depend on the accuracy of the hours, roles, and financials provided by the client. Hours are allocated from the owner interview; market wages reflect public listings on the preparation date and will move over time. BLS anchors are shown as approximate in this illustrative sample.

Nature of this report. This is a documented professional estimate of reasonable compensation prepared for Solux Media Corp using the cost (replacement-wage) approach and publicly available wage data. It supports the figure with occupational benchmarks and the IRS's recognized factors, but it is not a legal opinion, a formal business valuation, or a guarantee of any particular audit outcome. For contested matters or large exposures, a formal third-party compensation study is recommended in addition to this analysis.
