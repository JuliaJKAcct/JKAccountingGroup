Executive summary

The bottom line

A reasonable W-2 salary for the owner of Solux Media Corp for 2025 is $64,000, in a defensible range of $62,000–$78,000. The figure is developed from three independent approaches — a cost (many-hats) build-up, a market total-compensation check, and the income / Independent Investor Test — that converge on the same neighborhood. The owner's current salary of $42,000 sits below this range while distributions of $128,000 run well above it: the pattern the IRS most often challenges. We recommend raising the salary to $64,000.

Methodology

How we reached the number

We developed three approaches and reconciled them. The cost, or "many-hats," approach (primary for a hands-on owner-operator) prices each role the owner performs at a triangulated market wage, scaled for company size, and weights by hours. The market approach checks that total top-down against what an owner-manager of this type and size earns. The income approach applies the Independent Investor Test (Exacto Spring, 7th Cir. 1999) to confirm the salary leaves an investor a fair return. Each rate is set at the defensible low of the corroborated data.

Why the number is lower than a BLS-only report

For the two manager roles the government/BLS wage runs far above what Miami employers actually pay — ~$157k vs a ~$75k market for marketing managers, ~$101k vs ~$62k for operations managers — and size-scaling then pulls the management hat down further. A BLS-only build-up would have produced a salary near $92,000; triangulation plus scaling lands the cost approach at $62,000 — while staying defensible because multiple independent sources support each figure.

Market figures via web search of public salary listings (Indeed, Glassdoor, PayScale, ZipRecruiter, Salary.com) on the preparation date. BLS anchors approximate in this sample; a live analysis pulls the exact OEWS release for the state/metro and percentile.

Approaches 2 & 3 · Market and income

Two more methods, then reconciliation

Market approach. Top-down, an owner-manager running a ~$500k single-owner agency draws a labor-market wage of roughly $78,000 (small-agency GM / marketing-director proxy). Note: owner-earnings surveys that report $200k+ conflate profit distributions with pay and overstate the labor figure — we use the labor-only comparable.

Income approach — Independent Investor Test. After the proposed salary, the company still returns far more than an investor would require, confirming the salary is affordable and not excessive:

Reasonableness cross-check

Current salary vs. recommended

● Under-compensated — increase recommended

The current $42,000 salary is only 25% of the owner's total cash ($42k salary + $128k distributions = $170k) and sits below the reasonable range. A low salary paired with large distributions is the exact fact pattern the IRS recharacterizes (Watson, Fleischer). We recommend raising the salary to $64,000; net income before owner comp is $185,000, leaving roughly $121,000 for distributions after the recommended wage.
